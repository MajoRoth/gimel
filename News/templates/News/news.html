New Zero Day Exploit For Windows 10 Found

A zero-day (0day) vulnerability refers to a security vulnerability for which no mitigation or patch is available at the time it is disclosed or made public. Existing software patches are unable to properly defend against zero-day exploits, meaning attacks of this nature present a serious security risk to organizations. Until the underlying vulnerability is mitigated, a zero-day exploit is akin to a pathogen for which no vaccine is available.
